## 📝 Project Reports

### Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic
For this task, I designed an infographic using Canva, a free design tool. My design, titled "Digital Literacy Unlocked," covers three essential digital age topics. First, it defines digital literacy as the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information responsibly, noting it is much more than just using a smartphone. Second, the visual outlines safe internet practices. It advises users to lock down accounts with strong passwords and Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). It also warns users to spot phishing scams by avoiding suspicious links and reminds viewers to be mindful of their digital footprint. Finally, it details proper email etiquette. Tips include keeping subject lines clear, using professional greetings, and proofreading before sending. One thing I found challenging was condensing these critical concepts into short, impactful bullet points. Balancing visual elements with necessary text without making the slide look cluttered was a highly interesting design exercise.

### Task 4: Email Etiquette & Digital Communication
*(Email draft and Social Media Checklist are saved in the `task-4` folder).*

**The Impact of Poor Digital Communication:**
A classic example of poor digital communication causing a real-world problem is the scenario of a college student losing a prestigious internship offer due to an inappropriate tweet. Frustrated after a difficult exam, a student posted a highly profane remark about their university. Because their public profile was linked to their real name, the hiring company’s HR department easily found the tweet during a routine background check and promptly revoked the internship offer, citing a violation of their professional core values. This devastating consequence could have been easily avoided. The student failed to practice the "think before you post" rule. Instead of venting publicly on a permanent platform, they should have processed their frustration offline or ensured their account was strictly private.

### Task 5: Cybercrime & Prevention
*(Ransomware Case Study and Prevention Checklist are saved in the `task-5` folder).*

**Cybercrime Awareness and Personal Habit Changes:**
While researching cybercrime prevention for this project, I was most surprised by the sheer sophistication of modern financial frauds targeting college students, particularly the "Telegram task" and fake internship scams. I previously assumed that mostly older, less tech-savvy individuals fell for online fraud. However, the psychological manipulation, the illusion of easy money, and the professional appearance of these fake recruiters make them highly deceptive, even for digital natives. Furthermore, learning how easily a screen-sharing app like AnyDesk can be weaponized to steal OTPs was eye-opening. As a direct result of this research, the one habit I will personally change is my approach to public Wi-Fi. I will stop checking my bank balance or making UPI payments while connected to the open campus network.
